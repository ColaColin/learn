Describe how Gorden Coons Surfaces work
Four curves define the border of the surface: F(x,0), F(x, 1), F(y, 0), F(y, 1)
A ruled surface is a surface that can be swept out by moving a line in space. This is done via a simple linear interpolation like this:
$P_1F(x,y) = \begin{pmatrix} 1-y & y \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} F(x,0) \\ F(x,1) \end{pmatrix}$
$P_2F(x,y) = \begin{pmatrix} 1-x & x \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} F(0,y) \\ F(1,y) \end{pmatrix}$
A bilinear surface interpolates first on one and then on the other direction:
$Q(x,y) = \begin{pmatrix} 1-x & x \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} F(0,0) & F(0,1) \\ F(1,0) & F(1,1) \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1-y \\ y \end{pmatrix}$ The surface can then be interpolated over by adding the ruled surfaces along both axis and subtracting the bilinear surface:
$F(x,y) = P_1F(x,y) + P_2F(x,y) - Q(x,y)$
