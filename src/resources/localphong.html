Draw the local phong model that determines the color of a point of a surface. Define all variables.


$L_a$ ambient light density
$L_p$ light density of incident light
$k_a, k_d, k_s$ Constants controlling surface color for ambient, diffuse and specular light
Light is build from three components:
Ambient light: $I_a = k_a \cdot L_a$ is independent of the situation. It is globally constant.
Diffuse light: $I_d = k_d \cdot L_p \cdot cos(\theta) = k_d \cdot L_p \cdot (L \cdot N)$
Specular light: $I_s = k_s \cdot L_p \cdot cos(\alpha)^m = k_s \cdot L_p \cdot (R \cdot V)$

Overall light: $I = I_a + I_d + I_s$.
$R$ can be calculated like this:
$I := -L$
$R = I - 2(N \cdot I) N$

An alternative to calculating $R$ is the blinn phong model. Here use the vector H, which is located in the middle between $L$ and $V$: $H := \frac{L + V}{||L + V||}$
Specular light: $I_s = k_s \cdot L_p \cdot cos(\frac{\alpha}{2})^{bm} = k_s \cdot L_p \cdot (H \cdot N)$
$bm$ should be higher, typically four times, than $m$ in the phong model for a similar look.
