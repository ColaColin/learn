What is the base function of bezier curves? How is it called?
$B_i^n(t) = {{n}\choose{i}} t^i (1-t)^{n-i} $. Reminder: ${{n}\choose{i}} = \frac{n!}{i!(n-i)!} $
They are called Bernstein-Polynoms

What are four basic properties of this base function?
Partition of $1$: $\sum_{i = 0}^{n} B_i^n(t) = 1$
Positivity: $B_i^n(t) >= 0$
Symmetrie: $B_i^n(t) = B_{n-i}^n (1- t)$
Rekursion: $B_i^n(t) = t B_{i-1}^{n-1}(t) + (1-t) B_i^{n-1} (t) $

How is the bezier curvce defined?
A bezier curve of degree $n$ has $(n+1)$ bezier points $b_i$:
$X(t) = \sum_{i=0}^n b_i B_i^n(t)$

What is the name of the line through the points of a bezier curve?
Bezierpolygon

What are the tangents on $X(0)$ and $X(1)$?
$X'(0) = n (b_1 - b_0)$
$X'(1) = n (b_n - b_{n-1})$
