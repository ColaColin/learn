Barycentric coordinates for points within a triangle, or any other object, are always within [0,1] and sum up to 1. (Partition of the 1).
They can be calculated using area relationships like this: $\lambda0 = A(\triangle(p,p_1, p_2))/A(\triangle(p_0, p_1, p_2)))$
$\lambda1 = A(\triangle(p_0,p, p_2))/A(\triangle(p_0, p_1, p_2)))$
$\lambda2 = A(\triangle(p_0,p_1, p))/A(\triangle(p_0, p_1, p_2)))$

The area of a triangle can be found using the cross product:
Remember the cross product of 3d vectors is:
$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{pmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ a_3 \end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix} b_1 \\ b_2 \\ b_3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} a_2b_3 - a_3b_2 \\ a_3b_1 - a_1b_3 \\ a_1b_2 - a_2b_1 \end{pmatrix}$ It stands on the plane created by a and b and its length equals the area of the parallelogram created by a and b.
Half that area is the area of the triangle:
$A(\triangle(p_0, p_1, p_2)) = \frac{1}{2} | (p_1 - p_0) \times (p_2 - p_0)|$
By embedding the 2d points in the $z = 0$ plane and simplifying the area of a 2d triangle is:
$ \frac{1}{2} | (x_1 - x_0)(y_2 - y_0) - (y_1 - y_0)(x_2 - x_0) |$
